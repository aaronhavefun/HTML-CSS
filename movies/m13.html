Title: Lupin III: The Castle of Cagliostro

Release Year: 1979

Personal Rating: 10/10

Box Office Profits: The film had a budget of ¥500 million (approximately US$2.3 million).

Genre: Animated action adventure comedy

Review:

Lupin III: The Castle of Cagliostro follows the adventures of gentleman thief Arsène Lupin III. After successfully robbing a casino, Lupin discovers that the money is counterfeit. His pursuit leads him to the tiny country of Cagliostro, rumored to be the source of the fake bills. There, he attempts to save Princess Clarisse from the clutches of Count Cagliostro, who plans to marry her and seize the ancient treasure of Cagliostro. With his trusty associates Jigen and Goemon, Lupin embarks on a thrilling escapade filled with intrigue, action, and unexpected alliances. Hayao Miyazaki's directorial debut combines humor, suspense, and heart, making it a beloved classic in the Lupin III franchise.

Title: Princess Mononoke

Release Year: 1997

Personal Rating: 10/10

Box Office Profits The film had a budget of ¥2.35 billion (approximately $23.5 million)

Genre: Epic historical fantasy

Review:

Set during the 14th Century in the Muromachi period of Japan, Princess Mononke tells the story of Ashitaka, a young prince cursed by the hatred of a dying boar god. The god has been corrupted by an iron ball lodged in its body. Ashitaka embarks on a journey to find a cure for his curse and becomes entangled in a war between the gods of the forest and the humans who exploit its resources. Director Hayao Miyazaki masterfully weaves themes of Shinto spirituality and environmentalism into this visually stunning and emotionally resonant epic. The film's breathtaking animation, complex characters, and thought-provoking narrative make it a timeless classic in Studio Ghibli's repertoire.

Title: My Neighbor Totoro

Release Year: 1988

Personal Rating: 10/10

Box Office Profits: The film grossed over $41 million worldwide at the box office as of September 2019.

Genre: Animated fantasy

Review:

Set in post-war rural Japan, My Neighbour Totoro follows two young sisters, Satsuki and Mei, who move to the countryside with their father to be closer to their hospitalized mother. They discover that the surrounding trees are inhabited by Totoros—magical spirits of the forest. When Mei runs away from home, Satsuki seeks help from these friendly wood spirits to find her. Director Hayao Miyazaki captures the joys of rural living, animism, and the wonder of childhood in this heartwarming tale. Totoro, with its iconic design, has become a beloved cultural icon and the mascot for Studio Ghibli.

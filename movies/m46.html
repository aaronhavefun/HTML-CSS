Title:Shin Godzilla

Release Year: 2016

Personal Rating: 9/10

Box Office Profits: The film had a budget of ¥1.3 billion (approximately US$11.8 million).

Genre: Japanese kaiju, disaster, political drama

Review:

Shin Godzilla is a unique addition to the Godzilla franchise. Directed by Hideaki Anno and Shinji Higuchi, the film takes a fresh approach. When the Japan Coast Guard investigates an abandoned yacht in Tokyo Bay, they encounter a living creature that emerges from the ocean. This creature, later identified as Godzilla, wreaks havoc on the city. What sets Shin Godzilla apart is its focus on Japanese bureaucracy and politics in the face of a national crisis. As the monster evolves and adapts, politicians grapple with red tape and decision-making. The film recreates the horror of nuclear weapons through Godzilla's terrifying forms and atomic breath, emphasizing the devastating consequences. Anno and Higuchi's vision brings a thought-provoking and intense perspective to the iconic monster.

Title: Nope

Release Year: 2022

Personal Rating: 10/10

Box Office Profits: The film had a budget of approximately US$68 million.

Genre: Science fiction, horror, thriller

Review:

Nope is a captivating addition to Jordan Peele’s filmography. Directed by Peele, the film blends elements of science fiction and horror to create a unique narrative. The story follows siblings OJ and Emerald Haywood, who run a horse ranch in California. When they discover a mysterious and potentially dangerous presence in the sky, they set out to capture evidence of the phenomenon. What sets Nope apart is its exploration of humanity’s obsession with spectacle and the unknown. As the characters confront the eerie occurrences, the film delves into themes of exploitation and the human desire for fame. Peele’s direction brings a suspenseful and thought-provoking perspective, making Nope a memorable and intense cinematic experience.

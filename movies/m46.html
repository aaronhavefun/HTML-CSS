Review:

Shin Godzilla is a unique addition to the Godzilla franchise. Directed by Hideaki Anno and Shinji Higuchi, the film takes a fresh approach. When the Japan Coast Guard investigates an abandoned yacht in Tokyo Bay, they encounter a living creature that emerges from the ocean. This creature, later identified as Godzilla, wreaks havoc on the city. What sets Shin Godzilla apart is its focus on Japanese bureaucracy and politics in the face of a national crisis. As the monster evolves and adapts, politicians grapple with red tape and decision-making. The film recreates the horror of nuclear weapons through Godzilla's terrifying forms and atomic breath, emphasizing the devastating consequences. Anno and Higuchi's vision brings a thought-provoking and intense perspective to the iconic monster.
